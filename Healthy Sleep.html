Sleep Well, Live Better

Your guide to restful nights and energized days.

Tips for a Good Night's Sleep

  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule—even on weekends.
  • Avoid screens at least 30 minutes before bed.
  • Use blackout curtains and keep your room cool and quiet.
  • Practice calming routines like reading or meditation before sleep.
  • Limit caffeine and heavy meals late in the day.

Understanding Insomnia

Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that can make it hard to fall asleep, hard to stay asleep, or cause you to wake up too early and not be able to get back to sleep.

Common Causes

  • Stress and anxiety
  • Poor sleep habits
  • Depression or other mental health disorders
  • Medications and caffeine
  • Chronic pain or illness

Symptoms

  • Difficulty falling asleep at night
  • Waking up during the night
  • Not feeling well-rested after a night's sleep
  • Daytime tiredness or irritability

Tips to Manage Insomnia

  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule—even on weekends
  • Avoid caffeine and large meals before bedtime
  • Create a calming bedtime routine (meditation, warm bath, etc.)
  • Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet
  • Limit screen time before bed
  • Exercise regularly—but not too close to bedtime
  • Consider cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia (CBT-I)
